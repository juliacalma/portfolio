Problem

Predict maternal risk level (low / mid / high) from demographic and physiological features such as Age, SystolicBP, DiastolicBP, Blood Sugar (BS), Body Temperature, and Heart Rate.

Data & Tools

Dataset: Maternal Health Risk (1014 instances, 7 features).
Tools: Python (pandas, scikit-learn, matplotlib), Jupyter Notebook.
Cleaning: kept duplicates (to avoid major data loss); removed HR outliers only; no missing values detected.

Methods

  • EDA with summary stats, histograms, boxplots, pairwise plots by risk level.
  • Decision Tree classifier with 10-fold cross-validation.
  • Hyperparameters explored one-at-a-time: max_depth and min_samples_split.
  • Evaluation via accuracy, precision, recall, F1, and confusion matrix.

My Role

  • Implemented full EDA and visualisations.
  • Built and tuned the Decision Tree model.
  • Analysed feature importance and errors (confusion matrix).
  • Documented findings and recommendations.

Screenshots

Results (Decision Tree)

  • Cross-validated accuracy: ~72% overall.
  • Final tree: depth 5 (~33 nodes); reduced complexity vs. baseline.
  • Top features: Blood Sugar (highest importance), then SystolicBP; BodyTemp, Age, HeartRate, DiastolicBP followed.
  • Error patterns: model was strong on high and low risk, struggled more with mid risk separation.

Learnings

Careful outlier policy matters (removed HR anomalies only); class overlap drives mid-risk confusion; shallow trees (max_depth) and stricter splits (min_samples_split) improved generalisation; future work: class-aware metrics, resampling or thresholds to address mid-risk.
